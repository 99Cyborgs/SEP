# T2 Failure Geometry

## Purpose
This note explains how to interpret the T2 same-spectrum counterexample benchmark without overstating it as theorem closure.

## What Is Held Fixed
- retained slow eigenvalues
- fast decay scales
- benchmark horizon and subsystem partition

## What Is Allowed To Change
- slow-block geometry
- transient amplification profile
- pseudospectral proxy magnitude
- resulting held-out performance of L1, L2, and L3

## Interpretation Rule
If the matched normal and matched nonnormal branches exhibit similar nominal spectral separation but sharply different transient amplification, horizon behavior, or L3-vs-L1 fit improvement, the benchmark should be read as evidence that gap-only reasoning is incomplete in finite-horizon settings.

## Non-Claim
This benchmark does not prove the general T2 theorem. Its job is narrower: make the failure geometry concrete and reproducible.

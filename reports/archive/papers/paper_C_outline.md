# Paper C. Nonnormal Corrections And Failure Geometry

## Claim
Spectral gaps alone are insufficient under transient amplification, and a nonnormal correction is required for finite-horizon leakage laws.

## Minimal Theorem Content
- T2 corrected leakage law candidate.
- same-spectrum counterexample family where pure gap logic fails at fixed retained eigenvalues.

## Figure List
- same-spectrum different-transient plots
- pseudospectral proxy diagnostics
- L1 versus L3 held-out comparison

## Anticipated Criticism
Transient-growth corrections may look empirical rather than theorem driven.

## Response Strategy
Make the failure geometry the point: center the same-spectrum counterexample pair, show where L1 breaks despite matched nominal gaps, and use pseudospectral and transient diagnostics as mechanistic evidence rather than theorem closure.
